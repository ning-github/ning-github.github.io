About

After graduating with my Bachelor's in Neurobiology, Physiology, and Behavior from UC Davis, I worked at the UCSF Cancer Center, a world leader in treatment and research, and then at 23andMe, a rising startup offering the unique experience of personal genetic testing delivered through online reports and website features.

There, with everyday exposure to servicing a web product—from how it is created, implemented, and updated—I discovered and grew my passion for programming. From that point on, whether in classroom courses, self-teaching, or personal projects, my waking hours have been devoted to learning to become a developer. I have loved every minute of it (even those script-stopping. program-breaking, error-debugging moments) and have no intention of stopping.

In addition to college coursework in C++, I have completed the languages tracks on Codecademy. I am currently further teaching myself through various web resources, such as Eloquent Javascript, Coderbyte, CodeEval, and RubyMonk. I enjoy learning, playing with, and using development tools and environments, such as with git commands and Sublime shortcuts, often tapping StackOverflow and Youtube as helpful resources.
